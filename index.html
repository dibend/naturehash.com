Feasibility Study and ROI Estimate
Regulation D Rule 506(c) Equity Financing for Liquid-Cooled Data Centers and Cannabis Greenhouses

Executive Summary

This feasibility study evaluates a $25M Reg D Rule 506(c) equity offering to deploy liquid-cooled modular data centers in Colorado Springs, CO, using 10,000 Bitmain Antminer S21 Hydro units (2 EH/s) for Bitcoin mining and 500 Dell PowerEdge servers (100 TFLOPS) for AI workloads. Excess heat is transferred via radiators to 5-acre cannabis greenhouses equipped with top-of-the-line hydroponic systems (Botanicare Slide Bench, AeroGarden Bounty Elite, Gardyn), leveraging Colorado’s $2.2B cannabis market (2024). Powered by 70% solar (35 MW) and 30% natural gas (15 MW), the Colorado C-Corp is optimized for MED licensing approval, targeting a 27% IRR by Year 3 and a 4-year payback period.

Project Overview

Location: Colorado Springs, CO
Industry: Data Centers, Cryptocurrency Mining, Legal Cannabis Cultivation
Legal Structure: Colorado C-Corp, optimized for MED licensing approval, liability protection via corporate veil, indemnification, and compliance with Colorado MED and SEC regulations.
Objective: Integrate energy-efficient computing with sustainable cannabis cultivation using containerized data centers and radiator-heated, hydroponic greenhouses.

Market Feasibility

Bitcoin Mining: 28.5% CAGR through 2030, driven by cryptocurrency adoption.
AI Computing: $500B market by 2027, fueled by high-performance computing demand.
Cannabis: Colorado’s $2.2B market (2024), with demand for premium, sustainably grown products.
Renewable Energy: Solar costs down 62% since 2012; natural gas ensures reliability.
Real Estate: Colorado Springs offers 215M sq ft of industrial properties.
Competitive Advantage: High-altitude efficiency, hybrid energy, dual revenue, liquid-cooled modular data centers, advanced hydroponics with radiator heat transfer, Colorado C-Corp for streamlined regulatory approval.

Technical Feasibility

Modular Data Centers:
- Bitmain ANTSPACE HK3: 10 liquid-cooled containers (1 MW each, ~1,000 Antminer S21 Hydro units) deliver 2 EH/s. Cost: ~$1M per container (including hardware). Source: Bitmain product specifications.
- Dell Modular Data Center (MDC): 5 liquid-cooled containers (10 racks each, 10 servers per rack) provide 100 TFLOPS for AI, with PUE ~1.1. Cost: ~$2M per container (including hardware). Source: Dell Technologies MDC portfolio.
Heat Transfer: Liquid cooling systems pipe excess heat to radiators in 5-acre greenhouses, eliminating traditional heating costs and enhancing sustainability.
Hydroponic Systems: Top-of-the-line systems (Botanicare Slide Bench, AeroGarden Bounty Elite, Gardyn) with automated nutrient delivery, LED lighting, and climate control, yielding 10,000 lbs/year of premium cannabis. Cost: ~$500,000/acre.
Energy: 35 MW solar (70%) and 15 MW natural gas (30%) at $0.042/kWh (weighted: $0.03/kWh solar, $0.08/kWh gas) ensure efficiency and reliability.

Legal Feasibility

Corporate Structure: Colorado C-Corp simplifies MED licensing, requiring no foreign entity registration. Controlling Beneficial Owners undergo background checks per HB 23-1042 (no residency requirement). Filing fee: ~$50 with Colorado Secretary of State.
Compliance: SEC Form D, Colorado MED licenses, METRC tracking, Colorado Blue Sky laws, local zoning in Colorado Springs.
Liability Protection: Corporate veil, indemnification clauses, insurance (D&O, general liability, cyber), and strict MED compliance mitigate federal cannabis risks.

Financial Feasibility

- Greenhouses ($5M): 5 acres at $1M/acre (hydroponics: Botanicare, AeroGarden, Gardyn; radiators; MED compliance).
- Land ($2.5M): 50 acres, S Academy Blvd, Colorado Springs ($50,000/acre). Source: LoopNet, Crexi (2025).

Conclusion

The project is technically, financially, and legally feasible, leveraging high-altitude efficiency, liquid-cooled modular data centers, and radiator-heated hydroponic greenhouses. The Colorado C-Corp structure optimizes MED licensing approval and investor confidence, making this an attractive Reg D 506(c) investment opportunity.
